Homemade Peanut Butter and Jelly


When I was a boy my mother and I used to whip up this recipe on warm summer evenings, chilly winter days and even sometimes in the spring and fall too. Growing up in the fields of Illinois, you learn to enjoy the simple pleasures. To get satisfaction out of even the most basic of delicacies is a treat in and of itself, wouldn't you agree? Today (and any other day after you forget the recipe and come crawling back for more) I will teach you how to make the most mouth-watering sandwich mankind has come to know. The PB&J!

Mixings

  • Crunchy Peanut Butter (you can use creamy if preferred or if you have weak teeth)
  • Grape Jelly is best, but whatever you have on hand should work
  • Non Artisinal Bread

Fixings

  • A Plate
  • A Knife
  • This recipe requries at least one hand, yours or otherwise

Prep

First and foremost, there are NO strict measurements to this recipe, when I say slather, you go for the largest amount you're comfortable heaping onto the bread. I see people try to make this recipe into a health food, and if that is you, I'd recommend leaving this page right now. Start by getting two slices of your bread and placing at least one of the sliced sides on the plate per piece of bread. Now, did you remember your knife? That will be dipped into the jelly first, used in a scooping fasion and then slather that jelly onto one of the pieces of bread's sliced side that is still facing up. Going jelly first is critical in the success of the meal. I find one can still use the same knife to go for the peanut butter next because the jelly just slides off onto the bread, unlike the peanut butter which has a tendency to stick to the knife unless cleaned thoroughly. Now, a dollop of peanut butter will certainly do for your next slice, but trust me, you want at least two massive globs. The dispersion just works better, more substance more surface area coverage, you get the idea. Once you've layered both pieces with a satisfying amount of each ingredient, really take a moment to ask yourself if this is it. Is this the sandwich that will satisfy the carnal cravings of a person? Is it enough to satisfy MY cravings?? Probably. You may be asking, well what about the hands the recipe called for!? You've been using them the whole time, and I didn't have to even tell you! You're already cooking with the best of 'em and leaving behind the rest of 'em!


Some Things To Consider Whilst Preparing This Meal

  1. The type of knife you use can make a big impact on the outcome of this recipe. A butter knife is more than sufficient!
  2. There are so many varities of the ingredients used here, one could really elevate this to any level they so choose, just be sure to credit me!
  3. The freshness of the bread is very important if you want an edible sandwich. Make sure that stuff is soft.
